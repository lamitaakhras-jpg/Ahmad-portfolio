Projects

Highway Wind Turbine — Senior Project

Lebanese International University | Spring 2024 - 2025

Presented by: Ahmad Abdo Ismail and Youssef Mostafa

Instructor: Dr. Ali Shaito

Committee: Dr. Assadour Khanjian

Senior project focused on designing a highway wind turbine system to generate renewable energy from vehicle-induced airflow. The project studied wind turbine configurations suitable for highway environments and focused on practical power generation in low and turbulent wind conditions.

The project compared horizontal-axis wind turbines and vertical-axis wind turbines. Horizontal wind turbines offer high efficiency in steady, unidirectional wind, but require wind to hit the blades directly and usually need more space and yaw mechanisms.

The Savonius wind turbine was selected because it is a vertical-axis drag-type turbine with S-shaped blades. It captures wind from any direction, operates well in low and turbulent wind conditions, and is simple, durable, low-maintenance, and suitable for small-scale power generation.

  • Studied renewable energy generation from highway airflow.
  • Compared horizontal-axis and vertical-axis wind turbine concepts.
  • Focused on Savonius turbine design for turbulent highway wind conditions.
  • Prepared project documentation and technical presentation slides.
  • Created and reviewed CAD design concepts for turbine components.
  • Analyzed the suitability of the turbine for urban, highway, and off-grid applications.
  • Explored design requirements such as durability, simplicity, compact size, and low maintenance.

Tools Used: SolidWorks, AutoCAD, Microsoft Office Suite
