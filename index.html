Projects

Highway Wind Turbine — Senior Project

Lebanese International University | Spring 2024 - 2025

Presented by: Ahmad Abdo Ismail and Youssef Mostafa

Instructor: Dr. Ali Shaito

Committee: Dr. Assadour Khanjian

Senior project focused on designing a highway wind turbine system to generate renewable energy from vehicle-induced airflow. The project studied wind turbine configurations suitable for highway environments and focused on practical power generation in low and turbulent wind conditions.

The project compared horizontal wind turbines and vertical wind turbines. The Savonius wind turbine was selected because it is a vertical-axis drag-type turbine with S-shaped blades. It can capture wind from any direction, operate well in turbulent airflow, and is simple, durable, and low-maintenance.

  • Studied renewable energy generation from highway airflow.
  • Compared horizontal-axis and vertical-axis wind turbine concepts.
  • Focused on Savonius wind turbine design for turbulent wind conditions.
  • Prepared project documentation and technical presentation slides.
  • Created CAD design concepts for turbine components.
  • Evaluated the turbine's suitability for urban and highway applications.
